### Athero-Express

Since [2002](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10564-004-2304-6) we have collected over 3,600 #plaques in the #AtheroExpress. We paraffin-embed, cut, and stain these #plaques for commonly present cells and structures ('phenotypes'), and manually study #plaques through #microscopy¬†üî¨

[![No alt text provided for this image](https://media-exp1.licdn.com/dms/image/C4E12AQEKxYXXXmbToA/article-inline_image-shrink_1500_2232/0/1629710217847?e=1647475200&v=beta&t=dYp0Bl_MvCNszXrVayjgoehz2vMT-8fOHjdhx4PgP64)](http://doi.org/10.1007/s10564-004-2304-6)

### ExpressScan

But manual analysis is cumbersome¬†üò£üòìüòÆüí®. So back in 2014¬†üí´ \[MD/coder] Bas Nelissen created #slideToolKit: a collection of scripts that enable fast, reproducible, and automated processing of high-throughput whole-slide images #WSI for analysis with [CellProfiler](https://cellprofiler.org/).
